Icon Design - Duolingo Icon Set

Design Iteration


For icon design, the principle of recognition is important, so I have chosen 4 icons that appears the most in my research and should receive the most recognition among users. I took a somewhat minimalist approach. I have drawn them on my notepad so later I have something to draw on top of in illustrator.


Design Rationale


1. Solid Symbols - I have only employed the three solid shapes (rounded triangles, rectangles and circles). To maintain a consistent style, more complicated object is achieved through a combination of them. There are closed or open lines, no colour overlays or positive/negative interaction.

Balanced visual weight and visual unity - I have adjusted the height and width, so they are balanced in each line. They are also made pixel perfect so as to avoid blurriness.

Unity with product branding - I have chosen an iconic sign to represent the action of listening, reading, etc, as are most Duolingo’s icons on their website. The incorporation of a character with glasses is a stylistic choice to keep up with its lively and playful style.

Colour Palette


he colour choices stay the same, which are all from Duolingo’s palette (#58cc02; #ade756; #ffce8e and #ffc800). So do their lines weight, corner size, etc.
