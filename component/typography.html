Before and After Comparison:


Design Rationale


The first issue to fix is the superfluidity of information. This poster aims at a specific target constituency (Framptonwick), so the audience need not to be reminded of the details such as “Framptonwick Labour Party” or “Framptonwck Division”.

The second problem is lack of hierarchy and organisation. For a distinct hierarchy, I have used grids and colours to arrange the three pieces of information discussed. The main vote line “Arthur Cox” is put at the upper middle, stretching from left to right.

Overall, red is used as a main colour, pointing to the Labour party identity.
